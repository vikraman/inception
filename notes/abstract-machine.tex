\subsection{Terms}
We have value terms and computation terms (subject to term formation rules, which are provided elsewhere).
For the purposes of the abstract machine, we distinguish between full terms and partial terms.
Every full term is also a partial term.

Full value terms are:
$x$,
$\ast$,
$\lambda x . M$,
$(L , R)$,
$\PM ( (a , b) =  M , N )$
\newline
Full computation terms are:
$\RET (M)$,
$\PUSH (M , x.N)$,
$\APP (M , N)$,
$\VAR (M)$,
$\SUB (a.M, N)$
\newline
Further partial value terms are:
$(\_ , R)$,
$(L , \_)$,
$\flag{(L , R)}$,
$\PM((a , b) = \_, N)$
\newline
Further partial computation terms are:
$\APP(\_ , N)$,
%$\RET (\_)$,
%$\flag{\RET (M)}$

\subsubsection{Typing rules for values}

\begin{align*}
   \frac{x : A \in \Gamma}{\Gamma \VTS x : A}
&& \frac{\Gamma , x : A \CTS M : B}{\Gamma \VTS \lambda x . M : A \Rightarrow B}
&& \frac{\Gamma \VTS M : A \quad \Gamma \VTS N : B}{\Gamma \VTS (M , N) : A \times B}
\end{align*}
\begin{align*}
\frac{\Gamma \VTS M : A \times B \quad \Gamma, a : A , b : B \VTS N : C}{\Gamma \VTS \PM((a , b) = M, N) : C}
&& \frac{}{\Gamma \VTS \ast : \unit}
\end{align*}

\subsubsection{Typing rules for computations}

\begin{align*}
   \frac{\Gamma \VTS M : A}{\Gamma \CTS \RET (M) : A}
&& \frac{\Gamma \CTS M : A \quad \Gamma, x : A \CTS N : B}{\Gamma \CTS \PUSH (M , x.N) : B}
&& \frac{\Gamma \VTS M : A \Rightarrow B \quad \Gamma \VTS N : A}{\Gamma \CTS \APP(M, N) : B}
\end{align*}
\begin{align*}
   \frac{\Gamma \VTS L : \LABEL}{\Gamma \CTS \VAR (L) : A}
&& \frac{\Gamma , a : \LABEL \CTS M : A \quad \Gamma \CTS N : A}{\Gamma \CTS \SUB (a.M , N) : A}
\end{align*}

\subsection{Interpretation}
Types $A$ are interpreted as sets $\llbracket A \rrbracket$ and contexts $\Gamma = x_1 : A_1 , \dots, x_n : A_n$ as products of sets $\llbracket \Gamma \rrbracket = \llbracket A_1 \rrbracket \times \dots \times \llbracket A_n \rrbracket$ as usual.

Value terms $\Gamma \VTS M : A$ are interpreted as morphisms $\llbracket \Gamma \rrbracket \xrightarrow{\llbracket M \rrbracket} \llbracket A\rrbracket$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\llbracket x_1 : A_1 , \dots, x_n : A_n \VTS x_i : A_i \rrbracket = \pi_i$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \VTS \lambda x.M : A \Rightarrow B \rrbracket = \lambda \llbracket M \rrbracket$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \VTS (M, N) : A \times B \rrbracket = \langle \llbracket M \rrbracket, \llbracket N \rrbracket \rangle$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \VTS \PM((a , b) = M , N) : C \rrbracket = \llbracket N \rrbracket \circ \alpha \circ \langle \ID , \llbracket M \rrbracket \rangle$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \VTS \ast : \unit \rrbracket = \langle \rangle$
\end{enumerate}

Computation terms $\Gamma \CTS M : A$ are interpreted as morphisms $\llbracket \Gamma \rrbracket \xrightarrow{\llbracket M \rrbracket} K_{\LABEL}\llbracket A\rrbracket$.
\begin{enumerate}
%\item $\llbracket \Gamma \CTS \RET (M) : A \rrbracket = \eta \circ \llbracket M \rrbracket$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \CTS \RET (M) : A \rrbracket \;\gamma\; k = k \;(\llbracket M \rrbracket\; \gamma)$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \CTS \PUSH (M, x.N) : B\rrbracket \;\gamma \; k = ({\llbracket N \rrbracket}^{\sharp} \circ \STR \circ \langle \gamma , \llbracket M \rrbracket \;\gamma \rangle) \; k$ \newline \hspace*{\fill} where ${\_}^{\sharp} : (X \to K_\LABEL Y) \to (K_\LABEL X \to K_\LABEL Y)$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \CTS \APP(M , N) : B \rrbracket \; \gamma \; k = (\EVAL \circ \langle \llbracket M \rrbracket , \llbracket N \rrbracket \rangle) \; \gamma \; k$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \CTS \VAR(L) : \LABEL \rrbracket \; \gamma \; k = \llbracket L \rrbracket \; \gamma$
\item $\llbracket \Gamma \CTS \SUB(a.M , N) : A \rrbracket = \llbracket M \rrbracket \; (\gamma , \llbracket N \rrbracket \, \gamma \, k) \; k$
\end{enumerate}


\subsection{States}

States are pairs $(t ; k)$, where $t$ is a stack of partial terms in an environment (a pair of a partial term and an environment) and $k$ is a stack of continuations.
If the partial term at the top of the stack $t$ is a partial value term, then $(t ; k)$ is a \emph{value state}, otherwise $(t ; k)$ is a \emph{computation state}.

An \emph{environment} is a map from label variables to computation states and from non-label variables to full terms in an environment.
\vspace{10pt}
