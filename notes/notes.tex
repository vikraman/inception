\section{CPS-translation}
I summarise here what I think is happening in the CPS translation.\newline

Let $\mathcal{F} = \textsf{Set}^\mathbb{F}$, where $\mathbb{F}$ is the category of finite cardinals and functions, let $S : \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{F}$ be the free substitution monad and $K_V : \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{F}$ the continuation monad with response type $V$.
Because $S$ is strong, and $S(V) \xrightarrow{\gamma} V$ is the initial substitution algebra, there exists by \cite{kockDoubleDualizationMonads1970} a monad morphism $S \xrightarrow{m} K_V = V^{V^X}$ , which is defined as the transpose of the morphism $S \times V^X \xrightarrow{m^\flat} V$, defined by the following diagram:

              \[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=50pt]
  S(X) \times V^X \arrow{r}{m^\flat_X} \arrow{d}{\str} & V \\
  S(X \times V^X) \arrow{r}{S(\ev)} & S(V) \arrow{u}{\gamma}
\end{tikzcd}
              \]

We want to show that $K_V(X)$ has a substitution algebra structure.
We need to show that there exist two morphisms

\[
  V \xrightarrow{\vark_X} K_V(X) \xleftarrow{\subk_X} {K_V(X)}^V \times K_V(X)
\]
which satisfy the substitution algebra axioms.
\pagebreak

We define these morphisms by means of the substitution structure on $S$ and the monad morphism $m$, so that the following diagram commutes:

              \[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=30pt]
   & V \arrow{dl}[swap]{\var_X} \arrow{dr}{\vark_X} & \\
  S(X) \arrow{rr}{m_X} & & K_V(X) \\
  {S(X)}^V \times S(X) \arrow{u}{\sub_X} \arrow{rr}{m^V_X \times m_X} & & {K_V(X)}^V \times K_V(X) \arrow{u}[swap]{\subk_X}
\end{tikzcd}
              \]

\vspace{20pt}
\noindent \emph{I am not sure, whether the rest is correct, but this is what I think is happening:}\newline

\noindent We define $\vark = m_X \circ \var$.
We define $\subk = \ev^{(V^X)} \circ \varphi_V$, in the following diagram:

              \[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=60pt]
  S(X) \arrow{r}{m_X} & V^{V^X} \\
   &  {(V^V \times V)}^{(V^X)} \arrow{u}[swap]{\ev^{(V^X)}}\\
  {S(X)}^V \times S(X) \arrow{uu}{\sub_X} \arrow{r}{m^V_X \times m_X} & {(V^{(V^X)})}^V \times V^{(V^X)} \arrow{u}[swap]{\varphi_V}
\end{tikzcd}
              \]
where
\[\varphi : {(-^{(V^X)})}^V \times -^{(V^X)} \to {(-^V \times -)}^{V^X}\]
is the mate \cite[p.150]{leinsterHigherOperadsHigher2003} of
\[ \str : (-^V \times -) \times V^X \to {(- \times V^X)}^V \times (- \times V^X).\]
%
This means that the diagram above commutes if and only if the following diagram commutes:\newline

              \todo{Check whether this diagram commutes.}
              \[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=60pt]
  {(S(X) \times V^X)}^V \times (S(X) \times V^X) \arrow{r}{{m^\flat_X}^V \times m^\flat_X} & V^V \times V \arrow{dd}{\ev} \\
  ({S(X)}^V \times S(X)) \times V^X \arrow{u}{\str_{S(X)}} \arrow{d}{\sub_X \times 1_{V^X}} & \\
  S(X) \times V^X \arrow{r}{m^\flat_X} & V
\end{tikzcd}
              \]

\pagebreak
Assuming that the diagram above commutes, $\varphi$ is, according to \cite{leinsterHigherOperadsHigher2003}, then given by the following composite:

              \[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=60pt]
{(-^{(V^X)})}^V \times -^{(V^X)} \arrow{d}[swap]{\eta} \arrow{r}{\varphi} & {(-^V \times -)}^{V^X} \\
  {(({(-^{(V^X)})}^V \times -^{(V^X)}) \times V^X)}^{(V^X)} \arrow{r}{\str^{(V^X)}} & {({(-^{V^X} \times V^X)}^V \times (-^{V^X} \times V^X))}^{(V^X)} \arrow{u}[swap]{{(\ev^V \times \ev)}^{(V^X)}}
\end{tikzcd}
              \]
where $\eta$ is the unit of the adjunction $- \times V^X \dashv -^{(V^X)}$.
\newline

\noindent The morphism $\subk_X$ can now be written as the following composite

\begin{align*}
  &{K_V(X)}^V \times K_V(X) \xrightarrow{\eta} {({K_V(X)}^V \times K_V(X) \times V^X)}^{(V^X)} \xrightarrow{\str^{(V^X)}}\\
  &\quad{({(K_V(X) \times V^X)}^V \times K_V(X) \times V^X)}^{(V^X)} \xrightarrow{{(\ev^V \times \ev)}^{(V^X)}} {(V^V \times V)}^{(V^X)} \xrightarrow{\ev^{(V^X)}} K_V(X),
\end{align*}
which neatly corresponds to the CPS translation given in the slides:

\[
  \subk_X \mapsto \lambda \langle M : {(K_VX)}^V, N : K_VX \rangle . \lambda k : V^X . M (N k) k
\]
